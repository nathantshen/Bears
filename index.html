Bears are neat!

Bears are mammals that belong to the family ursidae. They can be as small as for feet long and about 60 pounds (the sun bear) to as big as eight feet long and more than a thousand pounds (the polar bear). They're fond throughout North America, Soth America, Europe, and Asia.

They have nonretractable claws, short tails, and excellent sense of smell. They'er typically solitary, except for mother with cubs. There aer eight species: Asiatic blakc bears (also called moon bears), brown bears (which include grizzly bears), giant pandas, North American black bears, polar bears, sloth bears, spectacled bears (also called Andean bears), and sun bears.

On average, bears can live up to 25 years in the wild and 50 in captivity. Six species, including the polar bear and the giant panda, are inclded on the IUCN Red List as threatened or vulnerable.
